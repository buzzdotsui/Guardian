🛡️ Internal Security & AI Policy

Mandatory rules for handling company data and using Generative AI tools.

⚠️ Why are you here?
If Guardian AI sent you to this page, it detected a potential leak in your recent Slack message (e.g., an AWS Key, a database credential, or proprietary "Shadow AI" usage). Please review these rules carefully to ensure compliance.

1. The Golden Rule of Generative AI

Do not paste any sensitive company information into public, 3rd-party Large Language Models (LLMs) like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or GitHub Copilot (Personal).

❌ NEVER upload or paste:
  • Proprietary source code, unreleased features, or architectural diagrams.
  • Customer names, emails, financial records, or PII (Personally Identifiable Information).
  • Internal API keys, secrets, connection strings, or `.env` files.
  • Financial projections, meeting notes, or strategic plans.

2. Handling Credentials & Secrets in Slack

Slack is a communication tool, not a secret vault. Even in private DMs or private channels, storing hardcoded secrets is strictly forbidden.

  • Never share AWS Access Keys, Stripe Keys, SSH Keys, or Database Passwords in plaintext.
  • If you must share a credential, use our approved internal password manager or secret-sharing tool (e.g., 1Password, HashiCorp Vault), generating a temporary link to the secret.
  • If you accidentally leaked a secret in Slack, deleting the Slack message is not enough. You must immediately rotate the compromised credential in your cloud provider.

3. Approved AI Tools

We absolutely encourage the safe use of AI to boost your productivity. However, you must only use our approved, enterprise-licensed tools where we have zero-data-retention agreements.

  • Use the company’s internal instance for coding assistance.
  • Do not install unauthorized AI extensions in your IDE or browser.

4. What happens if Guardian AI flags me?

Guardian AI is an automated agent protecting our data. If flagged:

  1. If it’s a false positive, click the ✅ Dismiss reaction on the original Slack thread.
  2. If it’s a true leak, click the ⚠️ Escalate reaction and follow the remediation steps in your DM, immediately rotating any keys or recalling the data.
  3. If you notice a security flaw yourself, use the /report slash command in Slack.
